Action Item A3 · Homepage Redesign

Usability Test Guide — SideDecked

Facilitator kit for 5–7 moderated sessions testing the homepage redesign prototype across four user modes.

● 5–7 Participants ● 20–30 min/session ● Lo-fi prototype · no polish
Step 1 of 4
Participant Recruitment
Target 5–7 people across all four homepage modes. Prioritise slots P01, P04, and P05 — these test the assumptions (newcomer, seller, community) that are hardest to validate analytically.
Priority order: P01 (genuine newcomer) · P04 (seller evaluator) · P02+P03 (returning buyers) · P05 (community browser). Slots P06–P07 are optional top-ups.
P01
NewcomerNever bought card singles before. Curious about TCGs or just heard the term. May play games but hasn't bought on a marketplace.
Required
P02
Returning Buyer — any gameBuys singles 1–4× per month. Has a wishlist or is tracking prices. Uses at least one other marketplace (TCGPlayer, CardMarket, etc.).
Required
P03
Returning Buyer — different game to P02Same profile as P02 but from a different TCG community (e.g. if P02 plays MTG, P03 should play Pokémon or YGO).
Required
P04
Seller EvaluatorHas listed cards for sale before, or is actively considering it. Wants to know if a new platform is worth their time. May also be a buyer.
Required
P05
Community BrowserFollows TCG content (YouTube, Reddit, Discord), looks up card prices sometimes, but doesn't buy regularly. Browses more than buys.
Required
P06
Power Buyer (optional)Buys frequently (weekly). Uses multiple marketplaces. Has strong opinions about UX. Good for stress-testing assumptions.
Optional
P07
Second Newcomer (optional)Same profile as P01. Having two newcomers increases confidence in P2 (orientation sentence) results.

Step 2 of 4
Session Guide
Use this tab during the session. Follow task order. Do not skip setup — the first-impression task (T1) is invalidated if the participant has already seen any part of the prototype.
Pre-Session Checklist
  • Prototype loaded on test device: _bmad-output/planning-artifacts/homepage-redesign-prototype-v1.html
  • Tab 1 (State 1 — Anonymous) active, scrolled to top, no tiles selected
  • Card-back tile images loading (check MTG / Pokémon / YGO / One Piece tiles are visible)
  • Screen recording running, or dedicated note-taker present
  • Stopwatch ready (for T3 time-to-first-value measurement)
  • Observation sheet open (Tab 3 of this guide, or printed)
  • Room quiet — participant comfortable, no one else watching
  • Verbal consent obtained: "OK if I take notes / record the screen?"
Intro Script

"Thanks for making time — I really appreciate it. Before we start, a few things: we're testing the design, not you — there are absolutely no wrong answers, and you can't make a mistake. Whatever you do is useful data for us.

I'm going to ask you to complete a few tasks on this phone. As you go, please think out loud — tell me what you're looking at, what you expect to happen, and anything that feels confusing or unclear. That commentary is the most valuable thing you can give us.

I won't be able to answer your questions during the tasks — that's intentional, because we want to see how the design handles it on its own. After each task I'll ask you a couple of questions. Any questions before we begin?"

// Handle questions briefly. Do not describe the site or explain any features. Hand over the device.
T1
First Impression
All participants · ~3 min · Do this first, before any other exposure
Tests P2
Setup: Prototype on State 1 (Anonymous), scrolled to top. Do NOT let the participant see it yet. Cover the screen or hold it face-down.

"I'm going to show you a website for exactly 5 seconds. Then I'll take it back. Ready?" [Show State 1. Start timer. Take back after 5 seconds.]

"What is this website? What can you do here? Who do you think it's for?"

Green signals
  • Mentions trading cards, TCG, buying/selling cards
  • Can name at least one game or card type
  • Uses words like "marketplace" or "shop"
Watch for
  • "I have no idea what this is" (P2 fail)
  • "Is this a game?" — confuses product with service
  • Describes the visual style but not the purpose
Follow-up: "Was anything immediately clear? Was anything confusing?"
T2
Trust Evaluation
Anonymous visitors + Newcomers (P01, P02, P03, P05) · ~5 min
Tests P3
Setup: Hand back the device. State 1 visible, scrolled to top. Let them explore freely.

"You're thinking about buying something here for the first time. Looking at this page — how confident do you feel about this site? What would make you more confident?"

Green signals
  • Reads or taps the trust strip ("8,400 cards · 340 sellers")
  • Asks about reviews, ratings, or return policy
  • Notices seller count as social proof
Watch for
  • Ignores trust strip entirely (P3 fail)
  • "These numbers feel made up" (P3 credibility fail)
  • Asks for reviews/ratings that aren't there (unmet need)
Follow-up: "Point to anything on this page that makes you more or less confident."
T3
Find a Card
All participants · ~5 min · Start stopwatch when task is given
Time-to-value
Setup: State 1 visible, scrolled to top. Start stopwatch silently when you finish the prompt. Stop when they tap the search bar.

"Find me a Near Mint Charizard Base Set. Show me what you'd do."

"If you were on this site right now looking for that card — go ahead."

Green signals
  • Goes to search bar within 5 seconds (search primacy)
  • Taps a Pokémon game tile before searching
  • Reads the search placeholder text
Watch for
  • Scrolls past search looking for a browse path
  • Time to first tap > 15 seconds (orientation failure)
  • Taps a trending card instead of searching
Record time: _______ seconds to first tap. First tap was: ☐ Search bar   ☐ Game tile   ☐ Trending card   ☐ Nav link   ☐ Other: _______
T4
Returning User Strip
Authenticated participants (P02, P03, P06) only · ~5 min
Tests P1
Setup: Switch to Tab 2 (Authenticated State). Scroll to top. Hand back device. Say nothing for 10 full seconds after handing it over.

"You've used this site before — you've got a watchlist and some price alerts set up." [Hand device. Wait 10 seconds in silence. Watch eyes, not the screen.]

"What do you notice first? What would you tap?"

Green signals
  • Notices the personalised strip unprompted
  • Can describe what each tile does without asking
  • Taps a strip tile as their first action
Watch for
  • Ignores strip, goes straight to search (P1 fail)
  • "What's this?" — strip not self-explanatory (P1 fail)
  • Taps strip but says "I'm not sure what this does"
Follow-up: "Without me explaining — what do you think those three tiles show you?"
T5
Seller Evaluation
Seller evaluators (P04) + any participant who answered Q4 positively · ~5 min
Tests P4
Setup: State 1 (Anonymous), scrolled to top. Let them scroll freely — do not prompt direction.

"You're thinking about listing your card collection here — deciding if it's worth your time. Spend 30 seconds on this page. Tell me anything that's relevant to that decision."

Green signals
  • Finds "Become a seller" below the trust strip
  • Notes buyer count / card count as demand signal
  • Asks about fees, categories, or seller tools
Watch for
  • Scrolls full page without noticing seller CTA (P4 fail)
  • "There's nothing here for sellers" (P4 critical fail)
  • Finds seller link only after 20+ seconds of searching
Follow-up: "What would need to be on this page for you to decide to list here?"
T6
Serendipitous Discovery
Community browsers + anonymous visitors (P01, P05, P06) · ~3 min
Tests P5
Setup: State 1, scrolled down to the Trending Now section. Hand device without comment.

"You're browsing with no specific card in mind — just exploring. Go ahead."

Green signals
  • Taps a trending card within 15 seconds
  • Reads card names / prices aloud
  • "Oh, that's interesting" — organic engagement
Watch for
  • Scrolls past without engaging (P5 fail)
  • "I'd only be here if I had something specific in mind"
  • Engages but says "I don't know what any of these are"
Follow-up: "What, if anything, would make you want to tap one of these cards?"

Decision Gates
If an assumption is invalidated (majority ✗), trigger the corresponding action before writing any production code.
P1 Fails
Revisit Concept 2 (Context-Restoring Homepage) architecture

If returning users don't understand the strip, the personalisation layer is not self-documenting. Options: add a one-time tooltip on first visit; simplify to 1 tile ("Continue where you left off"); or drop the strip and surface context inline with search. Do not build this in production until the strip is validated.

P3 Fails
Accelerate real-time data pipeline or find alternative trust signal

If static counts feel fabricated, swap to dynamic counts sourced from real data even at low volume ("47 listings added this week"), or replace with social proof (reviews, verified seller badges). Do not ship A-static metrics that users actively distrust.

P4 Fails
Elevate seller signal to hero level

If seller evaluators can't find the CTA without prompting, promote to a bifurcated hero (Crazy 8 C2): left column for buyers, right column for sellers. This is a larger layout change that requires its own design pass. Flag as P0 blocker before seller acquisition campaigns.

Synthesis Questions
  1. Which assumptions held across all participants who tested them?
  2. Which were invalidated — and what is the severity? (Cosmetic fix vs. architectural rethink?)
  3. What patterns emerged across tasks that weren't in any hypothesis?
  4. What is the single highest-impact change to make before the next prototype iteration?
  5. Are we ready to move to A5 (refine prototype), or do results require reframing the problem (back to DEFINE)?
